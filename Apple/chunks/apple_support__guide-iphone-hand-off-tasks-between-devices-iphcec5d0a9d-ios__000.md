# Hand off tasks between your iPhone and your other devices

With Handoff, you can start a task on one device and complete it on another. For example, you can start an email on your iPhone but then finish it on your Mac computer’s bigger screen.

You can use Handoff with many Apple apps—for example, Safari, Mail, FaceTime, and Maps. Some third-party apps also work with Handoff.

You can use Handoff between your iPhone, iPad, and Mac. You can also hand off tasks between your Apple Watch and iPhone.

## Before you begin

Make sure of the following:

- Both devices have [Wi-Fi](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph5f955a936/26/ios/26) and [Bluetooth](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph453f556d6/26/ios/26) ® turned on.

- You’re [signed in to the same Apple Account](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph39beaede9/26/ios/26) on both devices.

- Both devices meet [minimum system requirements for Handoff](https://support.apple.com/108046/#handoff).

If you’re using a VPN, make sure its configuration doesn’t prevent local networking, as that may interfere with some Continuity features.
