## Use Wi-Fi when you can

Wi-Fi uses less battery power than cellular networks. When you use apps that use the internet, make sure that Wi-Fi is on and connect to a Wi-Fi network if possible:

1. Open Settings and tap Wi-Fi.

2. Choose a Wi-Fi network.

If you can, use your device in a place with a strong signal. Both Wi-Fi and cellular connections use less energy when used in places with high signal strength. If you know you'll be in an area with no coverage for some time, [enable Airplane Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-us/108785).

With iPhone 12 models and later, iPhone SE (3rd generation), and [iPad models with 5G](https://support.apple.com/en-us/108787), you can turn on 5G Auto, which enables Smart Data mode. When 5G speeds don’t provide a noticeably better experience, your device automatically switches to LTE, saving battery life.

To turn on 5G Auto:

1. For iPhone, open Settings, tap Cellular, tap Cellular Data Options, and then tap Voice & Data. For iPad, open Settings, tap Cellular Data Options, and then tap Data.

2. Tap 5G Auto.

## If battery life is less than expected after an update

If you notice that your battery life has decreased after updating your device, wait a few days and then check again.

Even though you can use your device immediately after an update, certain tasks related to the update continue in the background and might affect battery life and thermal performance. While they're ongoing, if you open Settings and tap Battery, you might see the Insight [Ongoing iOS Update](https://support.apple.com/en-us/120745#Insights).

## Use Adaptive Power

With Adaptive Power, your iPhone can automatically extend battery life on days when you're using more power than usual. Adaptive Power can predict when you'll need extra battery and then make performance adjustments to help your battery last longer. When active, Adaptive Power can even turn on Low Power Mode to extend battery life automatically when your battery reaches 20 percent.

You can use Adaptive Power with iOS 26 or later and one of these devices:

- iPhone 17 models

- iPhone Air

- iPhone 16 models

- iPhone 15 Pro Max

- iPhone 15 Pro

[Learn more about Adaptive Power](https://support.apple.com/en-us/123707)
