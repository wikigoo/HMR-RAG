## Use a photo as a background

1. Go to the Messages app ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/ad1f1e874b79cd34473c266e98f9cbd0.png) on your iPhone.

2. Go to the conversation where you want to add a photo to the background.

3. Tap the contact or group icon at the top of the conversation, then tap Backgrounds.

4. Tap Photo, then tap to select a photo from your library.

**Tip:** If someone shared a photo you want to use as a background, touch and hold the photo in the message, then tap Set as Background.

5. Do any of the following:

   - _Crop the image:_ Pinch in or out.

   - _Remove the color:_ Swipe left to Black & White.

   - _Apply a color filter:_ Swipe left to Duotone or Color Wash, then tap the color swatch to change the palette.
6. Tap ![the Done button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/7c29db565673f106ec2610dbf4a90268.png) to add the background to your conversation.

You can set conversations to showcase backgrounds when you return to them. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/610900798ec772fa266e98f9f6042807.png) \> Apps > Messages, then turn on Start with Photos Visible.

If Apple Intelligence\* is turned on, you can use Image Playground to create one-of-a-kind images for the background of your conversations. See [Use Apple Intelligence in Messages](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/use-apple-intelligence-in-messages-iph64709c5c3/26/ios/26).

_Note:_ You can have your (or a family member’s) iPhone receive warnings before a background image containing sensitive content is sent or received. See [Receive warnings about sensitive content](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/receive-warnings-about-sensitive-content-iphede874992/26/ios/26) or [Check for sensitive images and videos](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/check-for-sensitive-images-and-videos-iph0d3607e18/26/ios/26).
