# Schedule a text message on iPhone to send later

If it’s too late at night or too important to forget, you can schedule a text message to be sent at a later time.

_Note:_ You must be using [iMessage](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/about-imessage-iph4e9799206/26/ios/26). Your recipient can use any device, and they won’t know that the message was scheduled. You need to be online to reschedule, edit, or delete a scheduled message.

## Schedule a message

You can schedule a message up to 14 days into the future.

1. Go to the Messages app ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/ad1f1e874b79cd34473c266e98f9cbd0.png) on your iPhone.

2. Go to the conversation where you want to schedule a message or [start a conversation](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/send-and-reply-to-messages-iph82fb73ba3/26/ios/26#ipha07bfaf56).

3. Tap ![the Add button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/2c6dba0cb486a1dee023b7a6ce137565.png), then tap Send Later.

4. Tap the time to open the scheduler, then choose when you want to send your message.

The border of the text field turns into a dashed line, and the time your message will be sent appears at the top of the message.

5. Enter a message, then tap ![the Send button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/9c754fcfdaf85ad3a4580697d1190816.png).

The message appears with a dashed line until it’s time to send it, and it’s delivered even if all your devices are offline.

_Note:_ Scheduled messages are encrypted and stored on Apple servers only until they’re sent. When a message is sent, it’s removed from Apple servers, the balloon becomes a solid color, and its dashed line disappears.
