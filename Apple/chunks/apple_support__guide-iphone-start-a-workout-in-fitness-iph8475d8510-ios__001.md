## Connect a heart rate device

Before you start a workout on your iPhone, make sure you’ve connected an Apple Watch, AirPods Pro 3, Powerbeats Pro 2, or Bluetooth®-enabled third-party heart rate monitors that connect to the Health app. For more information on AirPods Pro 3, see [Track your heart rate during workouts with AirPods Pro 3](https://support.apple.com/guide/airpods/dev1b40fb47d) in the AirPods User Guide.

If you do not have a heart rate device connected, do the following:

1. Go to the Fitness app ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/4a171706431000fd128f989d724dd5ca.png) on your iPhone.

2. Tap Workout, then tap ![the Heart Rate Devices button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/7493f45b28c115223f51d17eb3433aaa.png) in the top-right corner.

3. Tap Bluetooth Settings, then [pair your iPhone with a Bluetooth accessory](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/connect-bluetooth-accessories-iph3c50f191/26/ios/26#iph386380112).

## Start a workout

1. Go to the Fitness app ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/4a171706431000fd128f989d724dd5ca.png) on your iPhone.

2. Tap Workout, then scroll to the workout type you want to do.

To see more workout types, tap ![the Add button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/443066cf6168833f76d9d7d3a2b80897.png) at the bottom of the tab.

3. To start the workout, tap ![the Start button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/083d349f844bc6723f9159d4fbf3d0a9.png).
