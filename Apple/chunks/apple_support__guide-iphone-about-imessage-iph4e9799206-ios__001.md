# About iMessage

iMessage is a secure messaging service that you can use to send and receive text messages in the Messages app ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/ad1f1e874b79cd34473c266e98f9cbd0.png) on your iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, and Apple Vision Pro.

If someone sends a message to your email address or phone number using iMessage, you receive the message [on all your Apple devices](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/set-up-messages-iph3d039b67/26/ios/26#iph6fc6b7653) that are set up to receive messages sent to that email address or phone number. When you view an iMessage conversation, you see all messages sent from any device, so you can keep in touch with others wherever you are.

You need an [Apple Account](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iphfd5021a18/26/ios/26) to use some iMessage features. If you’ve made purchases from the iTunes Store or App Store or you’ve signed in to iCloud, you have an Apple Account.

![A conversation using iMessage.](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/d712294a4c21ba6b1d074d315b1dea74.png)

Some things to know about communicating in iMessage:

- You can send texts over Wi-Fi or cellular service.

- Texts you send and receive using iMessage don’t count against your SMS, MMS, or RCS allowances in your cellular messaging plan, but cellular data rates may apply.

- When you communicate with others who also use iMessage on an Apple device, the texts appear in blue bubbles. (SMS, MMS, and RCS messages appear in green bubbles.)

Messages are sent with iMessage when you see a blue ![the Send button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/f5dabff8f401af1580540b6ab7aedcb8.png) send button. A green send button indicates the message is sent with SMS, MMS, or RCS.

- In a conversation, you can see if someone’s writing a message. If someone has [read receipts](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/turn-read-receipts-on-or-off-iph5e713a045/26/ios/26) turned on, you can also see if they’ve opened your message.
