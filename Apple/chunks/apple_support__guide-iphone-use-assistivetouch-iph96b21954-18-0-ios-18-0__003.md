## Set up Dwell Control

iPhone performs a selected action when you hold the cursor still on a screen element or an area of the screen.

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Accessibility > Touch > AssistiveTouch, then turn on Dwell Control.

2. Adjust any of the following:

   - _Fallback Action:_ Turn on to revert the dwell action to the selected fallback action after performing an operation.

   - _Movement Tolerance:_ Adjust the distance the cursor can move while dwelling on an item.

   - _Hot Corners:_ Perform a selected action—such as take a screenshot, open Control Center, activate Siri, scroll, or use a shortcut—when the cursor dwells in a corner of the screen.

   - _Time needed to initiate a dwell action:_ Tap ![the Decrement button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/43215f37ef59b6605cde4d5da99f6161.png) or ![the Increment button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/1c855795afee486e11acd25121b275da.png).

## Use simple sounds to perform gestures

With AssistiveTouch, you can have iPhone perform a gesture or other action when you make a simple sound, such as a mouth pop or an S-sound.

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Accessibility > Touch > AssistiveTouch, then turn AssistiveTouch on.

2. Tap Sound Actions, then tap a sound.

3. Select the gesture or other action you want performed when you make the sound.

4. To perform the gesture or action, just make the sound.
