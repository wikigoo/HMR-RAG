# Take portraits with your iPhone camera

You can apply a depth-of-field effect that keeps your subject—people, pets, or objects—sharp while creating a beautifully blurred foreground and background. You can also apply and adjust different lighting effects to your portraits.

## Take a portrait

![The Camera screen in Portrait mode; in the viewfinder, the subject is sharp and the background is blurred. The dial to select Portrait Lighting is open in the bottom of the frame and Studio Light is selected.](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/df76d3049b2fdb62131de066b2bb1a5e.png)

1. Open Camera ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/71ce3044f246f5f1f828b819f1c395b6.png) on your iPhone.

2. Do any of the following:

   - Swipe left to select Portrait mode.

   - Tap ![the Depth button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/25cdab1e14eff2b794ff28c2bccd2ba7.png) (if a face is detected in the camera frame; on [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iphde3d86c94/26/ios/26)).

   - Use the Camera Control to select Portrait mode; see [Select and adjust the Camera Control settings](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/use-the-camera-control-iph0c397b154/26/ios/26#iphf10d97496).
3. Drag ![the Portrait Lighting control](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/3d400598bc5159bfe3fc68d86d0a0f2e.png) to choose a lighting effect:

   - _Natural Light:_ Sharp focus with a blurred background

   - _Studio Light:_ Bright, clean lighting

   - _Contour Light:_ Dramatic shadows with highlights and lowlights

   - _Stage Light:_ Spotlight effect against a black background

   - _Stage Light Mono:_ Stage Light effect in black and white

   - _High-Key Light Mono:_ Grayscale subject on a white background
4. Tap the Shutter button or click the Camera Control on ( [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph345af6a5d/26/ios/26)) to take the portrait.


After you take the portrait, you can remove the effect if you want to see the image without it. In the Photos app, open the photo, tap ![the Edit button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/2168728be5f88df306e409efc69488a0.png), then tap Portrait to turn the effect on or off.

Your iPhone captures depth information when ![the Depth button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/25cdab1e14eff2b794ff28c2bccd2ba7.png) appears while taking photos in Photo mode, so you can apply the portrait effect later even if you didn’t use it when taking the original photo. See [Apply portrait effects to photos taken in Photo mode](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/edit-portrait-mode-photos-iph310a9a220/26/ios/26#ipha7919eb64).

On [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iphbaefb01b6/26/ios/26), Night mode turns on when you take portraits in low-light situations with the wide (1x) lens. To learn more about Night mode, see [Take Night mode photos](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/take-night-mode-photos-iph1a3c5b4c3/26/ios/26).

## Adjust the background blur

Use the Depth Control slider to adjust the level of background blur in your portraits.

1. Open Camera ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/71ce3044f246f5f1f828b819f1c395b6.png) on your iPhone.

2. Swipe left to select [Portrait mode](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/camera-basics-iph263472f78/26/ios/26#iphac6618d52) or stay in Photo mode and tap ![the Depth Off button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/25cdab1e14eff2b794ff28c2bccd2ba7.png) on ( [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iphde3d86c94/26/ios/26)).

3. Tap ![the Camera Controls button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/8ed35ca4bcbec2cdd97804e0495a881b.png) or swipe up from Portrait mode at the bottom of the screen.

4. Tap Aperture, then drag the slider below the frame right or left to adjust the effect.

5. Tap ![the Camera Control Status button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/6b95c3060c7ea35acdffad3d68c54277.png) to close the Depth Control slider.


On [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph345af6a5d/26/ios/26), you can also adjust the depth control using the Camera Control. See [Select and adjust the Camera Control settings](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/use-the-camera-control-iph0c397b154/26/ios/26#iphf10d97496).

After you capture a portrait, you can use the Depth Control slider in the Photos app to further adjust the background blur effect. See [Edit Portrait mode photos](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/edit-portrait-mode-photos-iph310a9a220/26/ios/26).

## Adjust the Portrait Lighting

Virtually adjust the position and intensity of the Portrait Lighting to sharpen eyes or brighten and smooth facial features.

1. Open Camera ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/71ce3044f246f5f1f828b819f1c395b6.png) on your iPhone.

2. Swipe left to select [Portrait mode](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/camera-basics-iph263472f78/26/ios/26#iphac6618d52) or stay in Photo mode and tap ![the Depth Off button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/25cdab1e14eff2b794ff28c2bccd2ba7.png) on ( [supported models](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iphde3d86c94/26/ios/26)).

3. Drag ![the Portrait Lighting control](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/3d400598bc5159bfe3fc68d86d0a0f2e.png) to choose a lighting effect.

4. Tap ![the Camera Controls button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/8ed35ca4bcbec2cdd97804e0495a881b.png) or swipe up from Portrait mode at the bottom of the screen.

5. Tap Intensity, then drag the slider below the frame right or left to adjust the effect.

6. Tap ![the Dismiss Portrait Effect Intensity Control button](https://help.apple.com/assets/69F8EBBDF3B89A4F6E0C704C/69F8EBC43862495245036393/en_US/6b95c3060c7ea35acdffad3d68c54277.png) to close the Portrait Lighting slider.


After you capture a portrait, you can edit the Portrait Lighting levels in the Photos app. See [Edit Portrait mode photos](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/edit-portrait-mode-photos-iph310a9a220/26/ios/26).
