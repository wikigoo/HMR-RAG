# Set up a Focus on iPhone

Focus is a feature that helps you concentrate on a task by minimizing distractions. When you want to concentrate on a specific activity, you can customize one of the provided Focus options—for example Work, Personal, or Sleep—or [create a custom Focus](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/set-up-a-focus-iphd6288a67f/18.0/ios/18.0#iphf9f22c88c). You can use Focus to temporarily silence all notifications, or allow only specific notifications—ones that apply to your task, for example—and let other people and apps know you’re busy.

**Tip:** To quickly silence all notifications, [open Control Center](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph7c412b4db/18.0/ios/18.0), tap Focus, then turn on Do Not Disturb.

## Set up a Focus

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Focus, then tap a Focus—for example, Do Not Disturb, Personal, or Work.

For your Focus, you can set up the options described in the steps below, but you don’t have to set up all of them.
![A screen showing five provided Focus options—Do Not Disturb, No messages during calls, Sleep, Personal, and Work. The Share Across Devices option is on, which allows the same Focus settings to be used across your Apple devices.](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/e966b88c21db42304c7d61758d3e3330.png)
2. Specify which people and apps to allow notifications from during your Focus:

   - _People:_ Tap People, then tap Allow Notifications From. Tap ![the Add People button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/27bb9655dccf819f6b80069b98f42598.png), select contacts, tap Done, then tap ![the Back button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/672f0597b3bef6ad7262a337e288ceb4.png) at the top of the screen. You can also silence specific people by tapping Silence Notification From instead.

   - _Apps:_ Tap Apps, then tap Allow Notifications From. Tap ![the Add Apps button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/27bb9655dccf819f6b80069b98f42598.png), select apps, tap Done, then tap ![the Back button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/672f0597b3bef6ad7262a337e288ceb4.png) at the top of the screen. You can also silence specific apps by tapping Silence Notifications From instead.
3. Tap Options, then select any of the following:

   - _Show silenced notifications in Notification Center instead of on the Lock Screen:_ Turn off Show On Lock Screen.

   - _Hide notification badges on your silenced apps to prevent the distraction:_ Turn on Hide Notification Badges.

   - _Silence calls and notifications:_ Tap Silence Notifications, then choose Always or While Locked.

   - _Dim the Lock Screen during this Focus:_ Turn on Dim Lock Screen.

   - _Intelligently allow important notifications to interrupt you:_ If you have any iPhone 16 model, iPhone 15 Pro, or iPhone 15 Pro Max, turn on Intelligent Breakthrough & Silencing. Any notifications specifically allowed or silenced will always be allowed or silenced.
4. When you’re finished selecting options, tap ![the Back button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/672f0597b3bef6ad7262a337e288ceb4.png) at the top of the screen.

5. Specify the Lock Screen and Home Screen options:

   - _Choose the Lock Screen for this Focus:_ Tap the Lock Screen preview below Customize Screens, select a Lock Screen, then tap Done at the top of the screen.

   - _Choose the Home Screen for this Focus:_ Tap the Home Screen preview below Customize Screens, select a Home Screen, then tap Done at the top of the screen.



     _Note:_ To make changes to the Home Screen to customize it further for your Focus, see [Move apps and widgets on the Home Screen](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/move-apps-and-widgets-on-the-home-screen-iphd2fc8ce30/18.0/ios/18.0).

After setting up your Focus, you can return to Settings > Focus at any time and change any of the options you chose above.

## Turn a Focus on or off

You can [turn a Focus on or off manually in Control Center](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/turn-a-focus-on-or-off-iph5c3f5b77b/18.0/ios/18.0) or [schedule the Focus to turn on automatically](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/turn-a-focus-on-or-off-iph5c3f5b77b/18.0/ios/18.0#iph219573264).

## Add Focus filters

When you set up a Focus, you can add app filters that determine what information apps will show during the Focus. For example, you can choose which mail account or which calendar to use during the Focus.

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Focus, then tap the Focus you want to add filters to.

2. Scroll down to Focus filters, then tap Add Filter.

3. Tap an app, then select the information from that app you want to use during the Focus:

   - _Calendar:_ Choose which calendars you want to show during the Focus.

   - _Mail:_ Choose which mail accounts you want to use during the Focus.

   - _Messages:_ Choose which message conversations you want to see during the Focus—for example, only conversations from people you’ve allowed notifications from during this Focus.

   - _Safari:_ Choose which Tab Group you want to use during the Focus.
4. Tap Add to add the filter to the Focus.


_Note:_ Focus filters don’t get synced across devices when you have [Share Across Devices](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/set-up-a-focus-iphd6288a67f/18.0/ios/18.0#iphd5eeb63ff) turned on in Settings > Focus.

## Create a custom Focus

If you want to focus on an activity that’s different from any of the provided Focus options, you can create a custom Focus.

![A Focus setup screen for the additional provided Focus options, including Custom, Driving, Fitness, Gaming, Mindfulness, and Reading.](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/86c7dec606ea6379c4de7e58edb3025a.png)

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Focus.

2. Tap ![the Add button](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/12d48fa9a82b5d7e291da08f1769ba33.png) at the top right, then tap Custom.

3. Enter a name for your Focus, then tap Return.

4. Choose a color and an icon to represent your Focus, then tap Next.

5. Tap Customize Focus, then set up the options for your custom Focus, following the instructions in [Set up a Focus](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/set-up-a-focus-iphd6288a67f/18.0/ios/18.0#ipheb6ee12a0).


## Keep your Focus settings up to date across all your Apple devices

You can use the same Focus settings on all your Apple devices where you’re [signed in to the same Apple Account](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/aside/iph39beaede9/18.0/ios/18.0).

1. Go to Settings ![](https://help.apple.com/assets/683A54F5227CBBDB98051B96/683A54FC545676DF600F97D4/en_US/88b2400e45bcf521514b7252cbb2d959.png) \> Focus.

2. Turn on Share Across Devices.



_Note:_ When you turn on Share Across Devices and turn on Share Focus Status, your Focus can show that you have notifications silenced. For example, if you silence notifications when you [Set up a Focus](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/set-up-a-focus-iphd6288a67f/18.0/ios/18.0#ipheb6ee12a0), people who text you will see that your notifications are silenced. You can change this setting for specific people on their contact card.
