**Samsung Galaxy** ([Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_language "Korean language"): 삼성 갤럭시; branded in Japan from 2015 to 2023 only as **Galaxy** [^2]) is a series of [mobile computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_computing "Mobile computing") [devices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_device "Mobile device") designed, manufactured, and marketed by [Samsung Electronics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Electronics "Samsung Electronics") since 2009.

The product line includes the [Galaxy S series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_S_series "Galaxy S series") of high-end smartphones, the [Galaxy Z series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_Z_series "Galaxy Z series") of [foldable smartphones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foldable_smartphone "Foldable smartphone"), the [Galaxy A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_A_series "Samsung Galaxy A series"), [M](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_M_series "Samsung Galaxy M series"), and [F](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_F_series "Samsung Galaxy F series") series of mid-range and budget smartphones, the [Galaxy Tab series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Tab_series "Samsung Galaxy Tab series") of [tablets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tablet_computer "Tablet computer"), and the [Galaxy Watch series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_Watch_series "Galaxy Watch series") of smartwatches. The [Galaxy TabPro S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_TabPro_S "Galaxy TabPro S") is the first Samsung Galaxy-branded [Windows 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_10 "Windows 10") device that was announced at [CES 2016](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer_Electronics_Show "Consumer Electronics Show"). In 2020, Samsung added the [Galaxy Chromebook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromebook#Samsung_Galaxy_Chromebook "Chromebook") 2-in-1 laptop running [ChromeOS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChromeOS "ChromeOS") to the Galaxy branding lineup.[^3] The [Samsung Galaxy XR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_XR "Samsung Galaxy XR"), first released on 21 October 2025, is one of the first major extended-reality devices to feature the [Android XR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android_XR "Android XR") operating system.

Samsung Galaxy devices come with a user interface called [One UI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_UI "One UI") (with previous versions being known as [Samsung Experience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Experience "Samsung Experience") and [TouchWiz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TouchWiz "TouchWiz")).

The Samsung Galaxy smartphones arguably brought Android into mainstream popularity in the early 2010s.[^4] [^5]

## History

The [Samsung Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_\(2009_smartphone\) "Samsung Galaxy (2009 smartphone)") was launched in June 2009 as Samsung's first [Android](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android_\(operating_system\) "Android (operating system)") powered device. At the time, the brand's flagship smartphone was the [Samsung Omnia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_i900_Omnia "Samsung i900 Omnia") and its [successor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_i8000_Omnia_II "Samsung i8000 Omnia II"), powered by Windows Mobile.[^6] Omnia had been the second full-touch Samsung device running the TouchWiz user interface (after the [Tocco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_F480_Tocco "Samsung F480 Tocco")), but the Galaxy had an unmodified Android interface; the TouchWiz UI made its way to the Galaxy series with the [Galaxy S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_S_\(2010_smartphone\) "Samsung Galaxy S (2010 smartphone)").[^7] The Galaxy S and its successor [Galaxy S II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_S_II "Galaxy S II") became very successful, eclipsing the company's other lines and operating systems.[^8] During the decade, the Galaxy phones "became the company's most-praised products \[and\] also were among the best-selling [smartphones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smartphone "Smartphone") in the world." [^9]

## Software

Samsung Galaxy smartphones run the Android operating system under the [Google Mobile Services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Mobile_Services "Google Mobile Services") platform, however Samsung and third-parties have bundled various other software in them too. The [TouchWiz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TouchWiz "TouchWiz") interface was used until 2017, replaced by [Samsung Experience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Experience "Samsung Experience"). This was then replaced by [One UI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_UI "One UI") in 2019.

The company has created many apps and services under the Galaxy brand specifically for these devices - many of which come preloaded - including the [Galaxy Store](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_Store "Galaxy Store") which provides apps and customizations. Since late 2019, several [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft "Microsoft") apps like [Outlook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Outlook_\(mobile_app\) "Microsoft Outlook (mobile app)") also come preloaded [^16] on Galaxy as a result of a Samsung-Microsoft partnership.[^17] [^16]

### Security

Samsung has been reportedly caught quietly installing an Israeli [bloatware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloatware "Bloatware") application known as AppCloud in its phone lineup in the West Asian and North African regions.[^18] This app is known for collecting large amounts of sensitive personal data, without a chance to opt-out or uninstall the app without achieving root access due to it falling under the system app umbrella. This process of gaining root access would trip the [Samsung Knox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Knox "Samsung Knox") E-fuse and void the warranty in the event this is achieved by use of [bootloader unlocking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bootloader_unlocking "Bootloader unlocking"), which has recently become impossible on all Samsung devices running [OneUI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OneUI "OneUI") 8 or above, as the code for unlocking the [bootloader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bootloader "Bootloader") has been completely stripped from not only the settings app, but also the bootloader itself.[^19] [^20]

### Interoperability

Samsung Flow

Samsung have made several tools for making various Galaxy devices like phones, tablets and watches, work closer together. **Samsung Flow** is a feature allowing content to be synced with a PC, such as notifications, replying to messages and authenticating from a PC, and sharing content.[^21] It was announced in November 2014,[^22] released in a preview form in May 2015 [^23] with the final release in May 2016.[^24] Microsoft's [Phone Link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phone_Link "Phone Link") has been included on Galaxy smartphones since 2019.[^17]

Another feature named **Multi Control** allows Galaxy smartphones to be controlled with a [Galaxy Book](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_Book "Galaxy Book") keyboard and mouse, and [drag and drop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drag_and_drop "Drag and drop") file sharing between them.[^25] **Device Control** is another feature in the quick panel that can control [SmartThings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SmartThings "SmartThings") and other devices.[^26]

## Regional firmware variants

### Region locking and CSC codes

Starting from the [Galaxy Note 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_Note_3 "Samsung Galaxy Note 3"), Samsung phones and tablets contained a warning label stating that it would only operate with [SIM cards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SIM_card "SIM card") from the region the phone was sold in.[^78] A spokesperson clarified the policy, stating that it was intended to prevent [grey-market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grey-market "Grey-market") reselling, and that it only applied to the first SIM card inserted.[^79] For devices to use a SIM card from other regions, one of the following actions totaling five minutes or longer in length must first be performed with the SIM card from the local region:[^80] [^81]

- Make calls on the phone or watch from the Samsung Phone app [^80] [^81]
- Use the Call and Text on Other Devices feature to make calls

With the launch of the [Galaxy S8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_S8 "Samsung Galaxy S8") series in 2017, that process has changed. Due to the fact that many variants use a Multi-CSC, it will only work with SIM cards from the same CSC group. For example, an AT&T SIM card will not work on cellular-based Galaxy devices sold in Europe and other countries.

### Model numbers

Since September 2013, model numbers of devices in the Samsung Galaxy series are in the "SM-ABCDE" format (excluding the Galaxy J SC-02F, Galaxy Centura SCH-S738C, and SGH-N075T), where **A** is the model series, **B** is the device class, **C** is the generation, **D** is the device type, and **E** is the country/region that it is made for (if applicable). Prior to September 2013, the model numbers were in the "GT-XXXXX" format; they were also in the "SCH-XXXX", "SGH-XXXX", "SPH-XXXX" and "SHV/SHW-XXXX" formats.[^82] [^83]

## Over the Horizon

"Over the Horizon" is the trademark sound for Samsung smartphone devices, first introduced in 2011 on the [Galaxy S II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_S_II "Galaxy S II").[^99] [^100] It was composed by Joong-sam Yun and appears as music in the music library of most Samsung phones released since 2011.[^101] [^102] Prior to 2011, "Beyond Samsung" served as Samsung's trademark music track, while "Samsung Tune" was used as the default ringtone. The sound appears as the default [ringtone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ringtone "Ringtone"), as well as the sound when the phone turns on or off (a snippet is used), and as a notification sound. While the basic composition of the six-note tune has not changed since its inception, various versions of different genres have been introduced as the product line evolved.[^103]

While the first two versions were created in-house at Samsung, later versions were outsourced to external musicians. The sound has been covered by various popular artists who have released their own arrangements and remixes of the song, such as [Quincy Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quincy_Jones "Quincy Jones"), [Icona Pop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icona_Pop "Icona Pop"), [Suga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga "Suga") of [BTS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BTS "BTS"), and various [K-pop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K-pop "K-pop") artists.[^104] [^105] In Samsung's U.S. registration of the trademark for the sound, it is described as "the sound of a bell playing a B4 dotted eighth note, a B4 sixteenth note, an F#5 sixteenth note, a B5 sixteenth note, an A#5 eighth note, and an F#5 half note".[^106]
