# Manage data usage on your Galaxy phone
**[Samsung Galaxy phones](https://www.samsung.com/us/smartphones/)** can help you manage your data usage so that you don’t exceed your plan’s limit. Additionally, you can set limits or warnings in accordance with your billing cycle so that your phone will notify you before you go over.

## Set and manage mobile data limit
The Mobile data usage menu will not be available if your phone does not have an active SIM card, a mobile network connection, or is connected to Wi-Fi only.
Managing a data limit on your Galaxy phone can help you stay within your plan and avoid unexpected charges or speed reduction (data throttling) when your data limit is reached.
  1. Navigate to and open , tap , and then tap .
  2. Tap **Mobile data usage** , and then tap the icon (it looks like a gear) at the top of the screen.

## Mobile data usage troubleshooting
If you're experiencing issues with mobile data usage or network connection, try using Wi-Fi as a temporary solution. However, here are some steps you can take to troubleshoot and resolve the problem with your mobile data usage:
## **Check for obstacles and interference**
Ensure no physical obstacles or interference are affecting your signal strength, such as being indoors, near large metal structures, or out of your coverage area.
## **Check the SIM card or eSIM**
Ensure the SIM card or eSIM is activated and is recognized by your phone.
**Physical SIM card** :
  1. First, power off your phone. Swipe down from the top right corner of the screen twice, and then tap the icon. Tap , and then tap again.
  2. Next, insert the ejector tool that came with your phone into the hole on the SIM card tray. Push the tool until the tray pops out. : Be careful not to insert the ejector tool into any of the microphone or speaker holes along the edge of your phone.
  3. Remove the SIM card and inspect it for damage or corrosion. Reinsert the SIM card, making sure it is properly installed in the tray.
  4. On most models, the SIM card should be placed in the tray with the gold-colored contact facing upwards. : If you insert the tray into the slot while the SIM card is placed improperly, the SIM card may not be recognized.
  5. Reinsert the SIM tray into the slot on your phone.
  6. Now, press the button to turn on your phone.
  7. Navigate to and open , and then tap . Tap , and make sure the switch next to your active SIM card (SIM 1) is turned on.

You can check out to make sure you are removing and inserting the SIM card correctly.
