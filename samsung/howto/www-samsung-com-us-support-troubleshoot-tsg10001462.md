# Samsung phone or tablet will not charge
If your Galaxy phone or tablet is draining quickly and doesn't seem to charge at all, doesn't charge past 80% or 85%, doesn't charge when connected to a PC, or doesn't charge when connected to Samsung DeX. There are a couple of things that may be causing the problem. For example, you may simply need to replace a worn out USB cable, or make sure you're using the correct charger.
## **Under what circumstances is your phone or tablet not charging?**

Samsung phone does not charge while using DeX Pad or Station
Samsung phone does not charge wirelessly
Samsung phone or tablet is not fast charging
Do NOT use any other charger or third-party chargers because they can hinder charging and cause battery issues. The Samsung factory charger is required to properly charge the phone.

First, check if the option has been disabled. Go to > > . Turn on the next to Fast charging and Fast wireless charging.
Galaxy Tablets do not support Fast wireless charging.
## Check the charger compatibility.
  * Use a Samsung 25W, 45W, or 60W charger.
  * Use a charger that supports Super Fast Charger with:
    * USB-C Type connector.
    * PD3.0 PPS 3.3V~ 11V/2.25A or higher.

Test your device with another charger. If possible, test to see if your device works with an alternate .
## Verify the device's components.
  * The USB cable should be secured firmly in the charging port with very little mobility. If your device detects a poor USB connection, an error will be displayed.
  * Inspect the device, charger, and USB cable for dust or other foreign materials.

## Check for third-party app interference.
Boot your device into to disable third-party apps. If Fast charging works in Safe mode, remove third-party apps one by one until you can confirm that the problem has been removed.
If Fast charging still does not work, please visit a [**Samsung walk-in service center**](https://www.samsung.com/us/support/service/%20service%20center "Find an official Samsung Service Center.") or you can schedule a [**repair service online**](https://www.samsung.com/us/support/service/ "Samsung Online Repair Service.").

## Charge the device.
Charge your device using a known good Samsung travel charger, plugged in to a known working outlet for at least 1 hour. The battery charging indicator may take up to 10 minutes to appear on the device's screen if the battery is completely depleted.

Disconnect the device from the charger, and then attempt to turn it on by pressing and holding the button for 15-20 seconds.
If the device turns on, the device needed to be charged. Turn off the device and reconnect it to the factory travel charger until it is fully charged.

Samsung phone or tablet will not charge when connected to a PC
Do NOT use any other charger or third-party chargers because they can hinder charging and cause battery issues. The Samsung factory charger is required to properly charge the phone.
## Verify the device’s components.
  * Verify that the device and USB cable have no physical or liquid damage.
  * Inspect the device and USB cable for dust or other foreign materials.
  * Check the USB connections for corrosion and bent pins.
  * The USB cable should be secured firmly with very little mobility. If your device detects a poor USB connection, an error will be displayed.

## Verify the charger.
Samsung recommends that you use the travel adapter that came with your device. If you need to use the PC as a charger, we recommend that you power off your phone or tablet, so it will charge faster.
## Verify the PC USB port.
If the PC has an available USB 3.0 port, often marked in blue, then faster charging may be available. Try using this port when charging your device.
Some PCs may also have a designated charging port intended to provide higher power output. Check your PC's manual for details.
Samsung phone does not charge while using DeX Pad or Station
DeX accessories only support high-speed chargers. DeX mode or Screen Mirroring mode will not launch when using regular chargers with less than 9V/1.67A output. Battery packs are not compatible with DeX.
## Check the physical connections.
Check the DeX accessory's physical wire connections and make sure that all connections are securely plugged in. Inspect the wires and connectors for damage, and replace any damaged wires.
## Check the power source.
Unplug the charger from the DeX Station or DeX Pad, and plug it in directly to the phone.
  * If your phone is in Maximum power saving mode, it cannot launch DeX mode.
  * If the phone is charging slowly, the charger is probably incompatible with DeX. Switch to a high-speed charger with an output of more than 9V/1.67A.
  * If the phone is not charging at all, the charger may be defective. Replace the charger with a compatible high-speed charger.

## Restart your phone.
  * Remove your phone from the DeX Station or DeX pad, and restart it using either the Power button or the Power icon in the Quick settings panel.
  * Reconnect your phone to the DeX Station or DeX pad.

Samsung phone does not charge wirelessly
  * The following troubleshooting information only applies to Samsung wireless chargers.
  * Wireless charging issues with 3rd party covers on Galaxy phones may arise due to non-Qi2 or non-Magnetic Power Profile (MPP) wireless charging technology compatibility. For optimal performance, use Qi2-certified covers and MPP wireless chargers, ensuring proper magnetic alignment and fast charging up to 15W.

## Check device compatibility.
Ensure your device is compatible with wireless charging. Most Qi-Compatible Samsung devices can be charged wirelessly:
  * **Galaxy Note phones** : Galaxy Note20 5G, Note20 Ultra 5G, Note10+, Note10, Note9, Note8, and Note 5
  * [**Galaxy S phones**](https://www.samsung.com/us/mobile/phones/all-phones/): S25 series, S24 series, S23 series, S22 series, S21 series, S20 series, S10 series, S9 series, S8 series, S7 series, and S6 series
  * : Galaxy Z Fold, Z Fold2, Z Fold3, Z Fold4, Z Fold5, Z Fold6, Z Fold7, Z TriFold, Z Flip, Z Flip 5G, Z Flip3, Z Flip4, Z Flip5, Z Flip6, Z Flip7, and Z Flip7 FE.
  * : Galaxy Buds3, Galaxy Buds3 Pro, Galaxy Buds Pro, Galaxy Buds Pro 2, Galaxy Buds Live, Galaxy Buds+, Galaxy Buds2, and Galaxy Buds
  * [**Samsung smart watches**](https://www.samsung.com/us/watches/): Galaxy Watch 5 Pro, Galaxy Watch5, Galaxy Watch4, Galaxy Watch4 Classic, Galaxy Watch, Galaxy Watch3, Galaxy Watch Active, and Galaxy Watch Active2

Galaxy A series, XCover series, and Galaxy Tab series devices are not compatible with wireless charging.
## Place your phone correctly.
Place the device in the center of the charging pad and ensure there are no obstructions. The Galaxy Z TriFold, Galaxy Z Fold, and Galaxy Z Flip models should be closed with the rear side facing down.
## Remove third party accessories.
Remove any cases, skins, or protectors from your device. They could be blocking the wireless signal.
## Disconnect wired headphones or charging cables.
If headphones are connected to your device, it could prevent the device from charging. Disconnect the headphones or place your device in a landscape position. Do not use your device while it is wirelessly charging.
If a charger or USB cable is connected to your device, the wireless charging feature may be unavailable. Disconnect any cables from the device’s USB port.
## Use the correct charger.
  * If a charger or USB cable is connected to your device, the wireless charging feature may be unavailable.
  * Use only the AC adapter and USB cable that came with your device or wireless charger to power the wireless charger.
  * Fast wireless charging will only be enabled on devices that have been certified by Samsung and the Wireless Power Consortium. If you are using a non-Samsung wireless fast charger, check with the manufacturer or WPC for the charger's certifications.

## Check for software updates.
If your Galaxy phone experiences wireless charging disconnections or issues after a software update, check for additional updates. This may occur due to insufficient optimization for specific wireless charging pads and standards like MPP or Qi2. To check for updates, open on your phone, and then tap . Tap **Download and install** or**Check for updates**.
  * If an update is available, it will automatically download. Follow the on-screen instructions to install the latest update.
  * If no update is currently available, check back later.

If the phone or tablet does not wirelessly charge after performing the previous troubleshooting steps, please visit a [**Samsung walk-in service center**](https://www.samsung.com/us/support/service/locations/ "Find a Samsung walk-in service center."), or you can [**schedule a repair service online**](https://www.samsung.com/us/support/service/ "Schedule a repair service online for your Samsung product.").
